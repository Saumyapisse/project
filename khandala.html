About Khandala

Khandala is a beautiful hill station located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. It is renowned for its lush greenery, serene landscapes, and pleasant climate, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat amidst nature.

Places to Visit

  • Rajmachi Fort: A historic hill fort offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and lush greenery.
  • Bhaja Caves: Ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves dating back to the 2nd century BC, featuring intricate carvings and sculptures.
  • Kune Waterfalls: One of the highest waterfalls in Maharashtra, offering a refreshing experience amidst nature.
  • Shooting Point: A popular viewpoint offering stunning views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the surrounding valleys.
  • Tiger's Leap: A cliff-top point resembling a tiger leaping into the valley, offering breathtaking views and adventurous activities like trekking and rappelling.

Local Cuisine

Khandala offers a variety of delicious Maharashtrian and local delicacies. Don't miss trying out specialties like vada pav, bhajias, chikki (a sweet snack made of nuts and jaggery), and misal pav.

Transportation

Khandala is well-connected by road and rail. The Mumbai-Pune Expressway passes through Khandala, making it easily accessible by car or bus. The nearest railway station is the Khandala railway station, which is a major stop on the Mumbai-Pune railway line.

Weather

Khandala enjoys a pleasant climate throughout the year. Summers are mild, with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 35°C, making it an ideal destination for a summer retreat. Monsoon season, from June to September, brings heavy rainfall, enhancing the beauty of the lush green surroundings.
