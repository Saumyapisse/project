About Aurangabad

Aurangabad is a historic city located in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical monuments, and vibrant markets. The city was founded in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Aurangzeb and has since been a center of art, culture, and commerce.

Places to Visit

  • Ajanta Caves: UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves adorned with intricate sculptures and paintings.
  • Ellora Caves: UNESCO World Heritage Site comprising Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut temples and monasteries dating back to the 6th century AD.
  • Bibi Ka Maqbara: A mausoleum built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his wife, resembling the Taj Mahal in Agra.
  • Daulatabad Fort: A majestic hilltop fort with historical significance and panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Panchakki: An ancient water mill dating back to the 17th century, known for its engineering marvels and serene surroundings.

Local Cuisine

Aurangabad offers a variety of delicious Maharashtrian and Mughlai cuisines. Some must-try dishes include Naan Qalia, Tahri, Mutton Kolhapuri, and Hyderabadi Biryani. Don't forget to indulge in sweet treats like Imarti and Gulab Jamun.

Transportation

Aurangabad is well-connected by road, rail, and air. The city has its own airport, Aurangabad Airport, which operates domestic flights. Local transportation options include buses, taxis, and auto-rickshaws.
